The Acquisition Challenge

Government does not lack innovation. It needs faster pathways to adopt it.

Emerging technology is moving quickly, but traditional acquisition pathways can make it difficult for agencies to identify, evaluate, and award solutions at the speed missions require. RAM addresses that gap by creating a clearer route from discovery to assessment to procurement.

Discovery is fragmented

Government teams need better ways to find relevant, mission-aligned technology.

Evaluation takes too long

Lengthy paperwork cycles can slow promising capabilities before they reach the right users.

Award pathways are difficult to navigate

Innovators need clearer, more approachable ways to move from visibility to opportunity.

The Model

RAM brings competition, evaluation, and award pathways into one transparent environment.

RAM demonstrates how government agencies can identify, assess, and acquire emerging capabilities through a structured digital process. It combines commercial-first access, video-based submissions, measurable evaluation criteria, and marketplace visibility to help shorten the distance between mission need and award-ready solution.

Less paperwork upfront. More direct understanding of the solution.

Through concise video-based submissions, innovators can present their capabilities in a more direct and accessible format. Government reviewers gain a clearer view of the technology, the team, the use case, and the mission fit before moving into deeper engagement.

Process

From engagement to marketplace visibility to awardable outcomes.

A structured, transparent path that both government users and innovators can navigate with confidence.

  1. 01

    Engagement and Access

    Innovators and government users enter through programs, outreach channels, or marketplace opportunities designed to improve access and alignment.

  2. 02

    Capability Alignment

    Solutions are aligned to mission needs, technical areas, and relevant government offices.

  3. 03

    Solution Presentation

    Innovators submit concise video-based pitches with structured technical framing.

  4. 04

    Assessment and Review

    Submissions are reviewed against criteria with subject-matter expert input.

  5. 05

    Marketplace Visibility

    Qualified solutions become discoverable to government buyers through relevant marketplace pathways.

  6. 06

    Award Pathway

    Government users can engage, compare, negotiate, and move toward award using available acquisition authorities.

FAQ

Common questions about RAM.

What is the Rapid Acquisition Model?

RAM is a structured marketplace-based approach that helps government agencies identify, evaluate, and acquire emerging capabilities faster.

Who is RAM for?

RAM supports government buyers, mission owners, program teams, contracting professionals, and innovators seeking clearer pathways into federal acquisition.

How are solutions evaluated?

Solutions are presented through concise submissions and reviewed against structured criteria with technical and mission-focused assessment.

Is RAM only for defense agencies?

RAM is designed around national security and government acquisition needs, with a model that can scale across multiple agencies and mission areas.

What makes RAM different?

RAM reduces upfront friction by using concise solution presentations, transparent evaluation criteria, marketplace visibility, and existing acquisition pathways.
